What Is A Prediction Market?

A prediction market is a financial exchange where participants trade contracts based on the outcome of future events. Unlike traditional markets that trade past performance, prediction markets aggregate collective wisdom to forecast what will happen next.

The Core Formula

Price = Probability

Each share trades between $0.00 and $1.00

$0.25

= 25% probability

$0.50

= 50% probability

$0.75

If an event occurs, each share pays out $1.00. If it doesn't, shares expire worthless at $0.00.

🎥 Referencing the Video Deep Dive

Event:

"Will Apple release an AI-focused iPhone model by June 2025?"

The Setup:

Marcus watches the video deep dive embedded above, which discusses Apple's product timeline and competitive pressures. Convinced by the analysis that Apple will rush an AI phone to market, he buys "Yes" shares at $0.80. The market is pricing in 80% probability—but Marcus believes it's closer to certainty.

The Math:

  • • Shares Purchased: $400 ÷ $0.80 = 500 shares
  • • Deadline: June 2025 passes—Apple announces delay to Q4 2025 😞
  • • Settlement: 500 shares × $0.00 = $0.00
  • • Net Loss: -$400.00 (100% loss)

Key Lesson: Even at high probabilities (80%), events can fail. Marcus paid a premium for shares that were already expensive—limiting upside ($100 max profit) while risking the full $400. Risk management is critical.
